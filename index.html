About

I am a PhD student at Scuola Superiore Meridionale in Naples and a visiting PhD student at Universität zu Köln. My research sits at the intersection of quantum information theory, quantum many-body physics, and quantum gravity.

My overarching theme is understanding quantum complexity: how entanglement and magic, or non-stabilizerness, characterize the structure of many-body quantum states, and how symmetries, geometry, and holography constrain this complexity.

Research

Quantum resources

Entanglement, magic and their role in many-body quantum systems.

Quantum gravity

Holography, AdS/CFT, black holes, and the possible resource-theoretic interpretation of spacetime structures.

Mathematical structures

Differential geometry, topology, algebraic structures, and geometric methods in quantum information.
